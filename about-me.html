About Me

TL;DR: Supposedly adult man spends many hours over two weeks attempting to craft a personal website only to end up with ugly 90's-era blog post

Where to start?

A long time ago, well, 14 days ago, in a galaxy - never mind.

It's a tale as old as time - as the universe expands, so the scope does creep, and so on and so forth, until the bitter end.

When the admission project instructions said ‚ÄúBuild a website about yourself. Trust us, it's not that hard,‚Äù I really did trust that I could do it. I truly believed I could do it. Unfortunately, I was incorrect.

First, what even is 'the self'? What am I even doing here? These are questions for another day. All I know is that decades of living with ADHD have left me with interests that seemingly change with the wind. So what exactly should I include in a project about myself?

Idea 01: Nature and Outdoors

For the past couple of years, my most consistent interest has been being outside: camping, hiking and taking photos. But is there anything more boring that being forced to look at some random dude's mediocre nature photos? No. No there is not. When one manages to get a boringly adequate shot of a Greater Yellowlegs, it's a you-had-to-be-there kind of thing, correct?

Idea 02: Technology

So then I thought, how about when I first started being interested in tech and computers after my father bought a mail-order programming course which included a Timex Sinclair 1500. Yes! That could be interesting. And I'll theme it around the concept of magic boxes. From my first magic box: a Fisher Price Magic Show toy, and onward. So, I tried to find some good images of these various boxes. Mostly I could only find ebay-quality snapshots - so I thought I'll try Dall-E. Well, Dall-E's ability to render old technology is inversely proportional to the age of said technology, so it wasn't quite what I was looking for.

Then -- aha! I'll make then in Blender. I dabbled in Blender about couple of years back, following a few tutorials and a course on udemy.com. I mean, these old computers and stuff are just constructed of boxes, right? Easy! Maybe I'll even figure out how to make them interactive in WebGL! I open Blender only to realize that I've forgotten all the keyboard shortcuts, how to navigate in the viewport, or basically how to do anything at all. And that trying to do anything in Blender with a trackpad on my 13-inch MacBook is well, not fun.

When I opened Blender, the most recent file was ‚Äúnanskitchen_01.blend‚Äù which I started back in December 2022. I was intending to add festive elements then print it out for my sister as her present. But I ran out of time, or was distracted by a squirrel or something, and of course I didn't finish it. Oh! I know, I'll make a portfolio of a bunch of my unfinished projects.
